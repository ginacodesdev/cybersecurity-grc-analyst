## What Is SOC 2

SOC 2 stands for System and Organization Controls 2.
It is a framework developed by the American Institute
of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA) that defines
criteria for managing customer data based on five
Trust Service Criteria. It is one of the most
commonly required compliance certifications in the
technology and SaaS industry and is frequently
requested by enterprise customers before they will
sign a contract with a vendor.

If you work in tech, you will encounter SOC 2.
It is not a matter of if — it is a matter of when.

---

## The Five Trust Service Criteria

SOC 2 is built around five Trust Service Criteria
(TSC). Security is the only required criterion.
The others are selected based on the nature of
the services the organization provides and the
commitments it makes to its customers.

| Criteria | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Security | Protection of system resources against unauthorized access — required for all SOC 2 reports |
| Availability | System availability for operation and use as committed or agreed |
| Processing Integrity | System processing is complete, valid, accurate, timely, and authorized |
| Confidentiality | Information designated as confidential is protected as committed or agreed |
| Privacy | Personal information is collected, used, retained, and disclosed in accordance with commitments |

## SOC 2 Type I vs Type II

There are two types of SOC 2 reports and understanding
the difference matters a great deal in practice.

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Type I | A point-in-time assessment that evaluates whether controls are suitably designed as of a specific date |
| Type II | An assessment over a period of time — typically six to twelve months — that evaluates whether controls are operating effectively |

Type I is faster to obtain and is sometimes used
as a stepping stone to Type II. However most
enterprise customers and procurement teams want
to see a Type II report because it demonstrates
that controls are not just designed correctly
but are actually working consistently over time.
A Type I without a path to Type II is just a
very expensive starting point.

## How SOC 2 Audits Work

SOC 2 audits are conducted by licensed CPA firms.
The auditor reviews the organization's controls
against the selected Trust Service Criteria and
produces a report that describes the controls
tested and the auditor's conclusions about their
design and effectiveness.

The GRC Analyst's role during a SOC 2 audit is
similar to the audit liaison function covered
in the day-in-the-life section — coordinating
evidence collection, connecting auditors with
the right people, and ensuring that the
organization's controls are documented and
demonstrable before the audit begins.

---

## SOC 2 and Continuous Compliance

One of the realities of SOC 2 is that the work
does not stop when the audit report is issued.
The controls that were assessed must continue
operating effectively between audit cycles.
Organizations that treat SOC 2 as a once-a-year
sprint rather than a continuous program tend
to find themselves scrambling every time an
audit approaches.

Platforms like Drata and Vanta exist specifically
to support continuous SOC 2 compliance by
automating evidence collection and monitoring
control status in real time. A GRC Analyst
working in a SOC 2 environment will frequently
interact with these tools as part of the daily
compliance workflow.

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Trust Service Criteria (TSC) | The five categories of criteria used to evaluate controls in a SOC 2 audit |
| Type I Report | A point-in-time assessment of control design |
| Type II Report | A period-of-time assessment of control operating effectiveness |
| CPA Firm | The licensed accounting firm that conducts the SOC 2 audit and issues the report |
| Readiness Assessment | A pre-audit evaluation to identify gaps before the formal SOC 2 audit begins |
| Common Criteria | The security criteria that apply to all SOC 2 engagements regardless of which other criteria are selected |
