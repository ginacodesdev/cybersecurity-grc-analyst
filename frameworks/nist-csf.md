## What Is the NIST CSF

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework is a voluntary
framework developed by the National Institute of
Standards and Technology to help organizations
manage and reduce cybersecurity risk. Originally
released in 2014 and significantly updated in
2024 with the release of CSF 2.0, it has become
one of the most widely adopted cybersecurity
frameworks in both the public and private sectors.

The framework is designed to be flexible and
adaptable. It does not prescribe specific
technical controls. Instead it provides a
common language and structure that organizations
can use to understand, assess, and improve their
cybersecurity posture regardless of their size,
industry, or current maturity level.

---

## The Six Core Functions of CSF 2.0

CSF 2.0 introduced a sixth function, Govern,
to the original five. The six functions together
provide a complete view of the activities that
make up an effective cybersecurity program.

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Govern | Establishes and monitors the organization's cybersecurity risk management strategy, expectations, and policy |
| Identify | Develops organizational understanding of cybersecurity risk to systems, people, assets, data, and capabilities |
| Protect | Implements safeguards to ensure delivery of critical services and limit the impact of cybersecurity events |
| Detect | Develops and implements activities to identify the occurrence of cybersecurity events |
| Respond | Develops and implements activities to take action regarding detected cybersecurity incidents |
| Recover | Develops and implements activities to maintain plans for resilience and restore capabilities after an incident |

---

## Why Govern Was Added in CSF 2.0

The addition of the Govern function in CSF 2.0
reflects an important evolution in how the
framework approaches cybersecurity risk. Governance
is the foundation that makes everything else
possible. Without clear policies, defined roles,
and executive commitment, even technically strong
security programs struggle to function consistently.

The Govern function covers cybersecurity strategy,
risk management policy, roles and responsibilities,
supply chain risk management, and oversight of
the cybersecurity program as a whole. For a GRC
Analyst this function is where much of the daily
program management work lives.

## How the CSF Connects to GRC Work

The NIST CSF is one of the most practical tools
a GRC Analyst can use for assessing and communicating
an organization's security posture. Its function-based
structure maps naturally to the activities of a
GRC program and its plain language makes it
accessible to both technical and non-technical
audiences.

Common GRC applications of the CSF include using
it as a lens for conducting gap analyses, using
its functions to organize program reporting for
leadership, mapping it to other frameworks like
NIST SP 800-53 to build a unified control set,
and using it as a baseline for measuring program
maturity over time.

---

## CSF and Other Frameworks

The NIST CSF is designed to complement rather than
replace other frameworks and standards. NIST
provides informative references that map CSF
functions and categories to specific controls in
frameworks like NIST SP 800-53, ISO 27001, CIS
Controls, and others. This mapping capability
makes the CSF a useful integration layer for
organizations that need to satisfy multiple
compliance requirements simultaneously.

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Function | One of the six high-level categories that organize cybersecurity activities in the CSF |
| Category | A subdivision of a function that groups related cybersecurity outcomes |
| Subcategory | Specific outcomes that support each category |
| Informative Reference | A mapping between CSF outcomes and specific controls in other frameworks |
| Organizational Profile | A description of an organization's current or target cybersecurity posture using the CSF |
| Tier | A characterization of an organization's cybersecurity risk management practices ranging from Partial to Adaptive |
