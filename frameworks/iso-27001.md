## What Is ISO 27001

ISO 27001 is the international standard for
Information Security Management Systems (ISMS).
Published by the International Organization for
Standardization and the International Electrotechnical
Commission, it provides a systematic approach to
managing sensitive information and ensuring its
confidentiality, integrity, and availability.

Unlike NIST frameworks which are primarily
developed for and adopted by US organizations,
ISO 27001 is a globally recognized standard
used by organizations in nearly every country
and industry. Achieving ISO 27001 certification
demonstrates to customers, partners, and
regulators that an organization has implemented
a rigorous and independently verified approach
to information security management.

---

## What Is an ISMS

An Information Security Management System is
not just a collection of technical controls.
It is a systematic framework for managing
information security that encompasses policies,
processes, people, and technology in an
integrated and continuously improving program.

The ISMS approach is built on the Plan-Do-Check-Act
(PDCA) cycle which ensures that security management
is not a one-time implementation but an ongoing
program of assessment, improvement, and adaptation.

| PDCA Stage | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Plan | Establish the ISMS scope, policy, objectives, and risk assessment process |
| Do | Implement the controls and processes defined in the planning stage |
| Check | Monitor, measure, and audit the ISMS to assess its effectiveness |
| Act | Take corrective and preventive actions based on the results of monitoring and auditing |

## The Structure of ISO 27001

ISO 27001 is organized into clauses that define
the requirements for establishing, implementing,
maintaining, and continually improving an ISMS.
The standard also includes Annex A which provides
a reference set of controls that organizations
can select based on their risk assessment results.

The 2022 update reorganized Annex A from 114
controls across 14 domains to 93 controls across
four themes:

| Theme | Focus Area |
|-------|-----------|
| Organizational Controls | Policies, roles, responsibilities, and governance |
| People Controls | Screening, training, and personnel security |
| Physical Controls | Physical security of facilities and equipment |
| Technological Controls | Technical security measures for systems and data |

## ISO 27001 Certification

Organizations can seek formal certification against
ISO 27001 through an accredited third-party
certification body. The certification process
involves a Stage 1 audit that reviews documentation
and readiness followed by a Stage 2 audit that
assesses the actual implementation and effectiveness
of the ISMS. Certification is valid for three
years and requires annual surveillance audits
to maintain.

Achieving and maintaining ISO 27001 certification
is a significant undertaking that requires sustained
organizational commitment. For a GRC Analyst working
toward or supporting an ISO 27001 certification
program, understanding the audit process and the
documentation requirements is essential.

## How ISO 27001 Connects to GRC Work

ISO 27001 is highly relevant to GRC work across
multiple dimensions. Its risk assessment requirement
aligns with the risk management activities at the
core of any GRC program. Its control framework
in Annex A provides a structured set of security
measures that can be mapped to other frameworks
including NIST SP 800-53. Its emphasis on documented
policies and procedures aligns directly with the
governance responsibilities of a GRC Analyst.

Organizations pursuing ISO 27001 certification
often leverage GRC tools to manage their control
inventory, track evidence, and monitor compliance
status. A GRC Analyst with ISO 27001 knowledge
is well-positioned to support or lead these programs.

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| ISMS | Information Security Management System — a systematic approach to managing information security |
| Annex A | The reference control set included in ISO 27001 |
| Certification Body | An accredited third-party organization that conducts ISO 27001 audits and issues certifications |
| Stage 1 Audit | Documentation review to assess readiness for the Stage 2 audit |
| Stage 2 Audit | On-site assessment of ISMS implementation and effectiveness |
| Surveillance Audit | Annual audit conducted during the three-year certification cycle to verify ongoing compliance |
| PDCA | Plan-Do-Check-Act — the continuous improvement cycle underlying the ISMS approach |
