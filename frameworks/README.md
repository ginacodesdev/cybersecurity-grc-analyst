## Overview

Frameworks and standards are the backbone of GRC work.
They provide the structure that organizations use to
build, assess, and improve their security programs.
A GRC Analyst who understands the major frameworks
(what they cover, how they differ, and when each
one applies)is equipped to work effectively across
a wide range of industries, regulatory environments,
and organizational contexts.

This section covers the frameworks and standards
most commonly encountered in GRC Analyst roles.
Each framework is explained in practical terms
with a focus on what it means for the day-to-day
work of a compliance professional.

## How to Use This Section

Each framework page covers what the framework is,
who it applies to, what its core requirements are,
how it connects to the work of a GRC Analyst, and
how it relates to other frameworks covered in this
section. The goal is not to reproduce the full text
of any framework. All of the frameworks listed here
are publicly available through their respective
governing bodies and should always be consulted
directly for authoritative guidance.

The goal is to build a working understanding of
each framework that is practical, accessible, and
immediately useful for someone entering or working
in a GRC Analyst role.

## Framework Relationships

No organization operates under a single framework
in isolation. Most compliance programs involve
multiple overlapping requirements. Understanding
how frameworks relate to each other helps a GRC
Analyst avoid duplicating effort, identify control
overlaps, and build a unified compliance program
that satisfies multiple requirements efficiently.

| Framework | Primary Audience | Voluntary or Mandatory |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| NIST CSF | All organizations | Voluntary (mandatory for some federal) |
| NIST SP 800-53 | Federal agencies and contractors | Mandatory for federal |
| ISO 27001 | Global organizations | Voluntary certification |
| SOC 2 | Technology and SaaS companies | Voluntary (often contractually required) |
| HIPAA | Healthcare organizations and business associates | Mandatory |
| PCI DSS | Organizations handling payment card data | Mandatory for card processing |
| FedRAMP | Cloud service providers serving federal agencies | Mandatory for federal cloud |
| CMMC | Defense industrial base contractors | Mandatory for DoD contracts |
| CIS Controls | All organizations | Voluntary |
