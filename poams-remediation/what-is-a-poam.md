# What Is a POAM

![Last Reviewed](https://img.shields.io/badge/Last%20Reviewed-March%202026-green)

---

## Definition

A Plan of Action and Milestones (POAM) is a formal
document used to track security weaknesses identified
in an information system and the organization's plan
to address them. Every weakness that cannot be
remediated immediately must be captured in a POAM.
The document serves as the official record of what
the weakness is, why it exists, what is being done
about it, and when it is expected to be resolved.

POAMs are not a sign of failure. Every mature security
program has them. They are a sign that the organization
is aware of its risks and is actively managing them
in a documented and accountable way.

---

## Required Components of a POAM

A well-structured POAM must contain specific information
to be considered complete and audit-ready. The following
components are standard across most frameworks and
federal requirements:

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Weakness Description | A clear explanation of what the security weakness is |
| Control Mapping | The specific security control or requirement that the weakness relates to |
| Risk Level | The assessed severity of the weakness — typically Low, Moderate, or High |
| Point of Contact | The individual or team responsible for remediating the weakness |
| Resources Required | Personnel, tools, funding, or other resources needed for remediation |
| Milestones | Specific, time-bound steps that describe how the weakness will be remediated |
| Scheduled Completion Date | The anticipated date by which the weakness will be fully remediated |
| Status | The current state of the POAM — Open, In Progress, Delayed, or Closed |

---

## Types of POAMs

Not all POAMs are created equal. The nature of the
weakness and the organization's response to it
determines what type of POAM is opened.

| Type | When It Is Used |
|------|----------------|
| Remediation POAM | The weakness can be fixed and the organization has a plan to do so |
| Risk Acceptance POAM | The weakness cannot be remediated and leadership formally accepts the associated risk |
| Operational Requirement POAM | The weakness exists because of a documented operational need that outweighs the risk |

Risk acceptance POAMs require formal sign-off from
an authorizing official or senior leader. They
acknowledge that the organization is aware of the
risk and has made a deliberate decision to accept
it rather than remediate it. This is not ideal
but it is sometimes the practical reality of
operating complex systems with real-world constraints.

---

## Where POAMs Come From

POAMs can be generated from multiple sources across
the security program. Understanding where they
originate helps a GRC Analyst stay ahead of the
workload and anticipate new POAMs before they
become surprises.

| Source | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Security Assessments | Findings from Step 5 of the RMF that could not be immediately remediated |
| Continuous Monitoring | Vulnerabilities identified during ongoing monitoring activities |
| Vulnerability Scans | Automated scans that identify unpatched or misconfigured systems |
| Audit Findings | Issues identified during internal or external audits |
| Incident Reviews | Weaknesses uncovered during post-incident analysis |
| Self-Identified Weaknesses | Issues that the system team identifies and proactively reports |

---

## The Lifecycle of a POAM

A POAM does not end when it is opened. It follows
a lifecycle from identification through closure and
every stage requires active management from the
GRC Analyst.

Opening: The weakness is identified and documented
with all required components. A point of contact
is assigned and milestones are established.

Active Management: The GRC Analyst tracks progress
against milestones, communicates with the responsible
party, and updates the POAM as work progresses.

Delay Management: When remediation falls behind
schedule the GRC Analyst investigates the cause,
documents the reason for the delay, and works
with the responsible party to establish a revised
timeline.

Closure: The weakness has been fully remediated.
Evidence of remediation is collected and documented.
The POAM is marked closed and retained for audit
purposes.

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| POAM | Plan of Action and Milestones — a document tracking a security weakness and the plan to address it |
| Weakness | A gap or deficiency in a security control or requirement |
| Milestone | A specific, time-bound action that describes a step toward remediating a weakness |
| Risk Acceptance | A formal decision by leadership to accept a known risk rather than remediate it |
| Remediation | The process of fixing or mitigating a security weakness |
| Scheduled Completion Date | The target date by which a POAM weakness is expected to be fully resolved |
