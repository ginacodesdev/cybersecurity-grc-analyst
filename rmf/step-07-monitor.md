## Overview

Receiving an Authorization to Operate is a significant
milestone but it is not the end of the story. Step 7
of the RMF is Monitor and it is the step that never
truly ends. Threats evolve every day. Systems change.
New vulnerabilities are discovered. Controls that
were effective at authorization may become insufficient
over time. Step 7 exists to ensure that the security
posture established during Steps 1 through 6 is
maintained, not just on day one but day after day
and year after year.

---

## What Continuous Monitoring Means in Practice

Continuous monitoring is not a single activity. It
is an ongoing program of activities designed to
provide real-time or near-real-time awareness of
the system's security posture. For a GRC Analyst
this means consistently coordinating with system
administrators and IT security stakeholders to
ensure that controls remain effective, that new
vulnerabilities are identified and addressed
promptly, and that the system's documentation
remains current and accurate.

---

## Core Activities in Step 7

| Activity | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Control Monitoring | Regularly verifying that implemented controls are still functioning as intended |
| Vulnerability Management | Identifying, tracking, and remediating system vulnerabilities on an ongoing basis |
| POAM Management | Tracking the status of existing POAMs and opening new ones when weaknesses are identified |
| Patch Management | Ensuring the system remains current with security patches and updates |
| Incident Response Coordination | Responding quickly when something goes wrong and documenting what happened |
| Documentation Maintenance | Keeping the System Security and Privacy Plan and other key documents current |
| Reporting | Providing regular updates to leadership and stakeholders on the system's security posture |

---

## POAMs Never Go Away

One of the realities of Step 7 is that Plans of
Action and Milestones do not disappear after
authorization. Existing POAMs must be actively
tracked and updated as remediation progresses.
New weaknesses discovered during monitoring will
generate new POAMs. A GRC Analyst working in
continuous monitoring is always managing a living
inventory of known weaknesses and the plans to
address them. The goal is not to have zero POAMs. The goal is to have POAMs that are accurate,
current, and moving toward resolution.

---

## The ATO Can Be Revoked

Authorization to Operate is not permanent by default.
An ATO can be revoked if the system's risk posture
deteriorates to an unacceptable level. This can
happen as a result of a significant security
incident, the discovery of critical unmitigated
vulnerabilities, failure to maintain required
controls, or changes to the system that materially
alter its risk profile. The possibility of
revocation is one of the primary reasons that
continuous monitoring must be taken seriously
and not treated as a formality.

---

## The Role of the GRC Analyst in Step 7

For many GRC Analysts, Step 7 is where the majority
of their day-to-day work actually lives. While the
earlier steps of the RMF are project-based with
defined endpoints, monitoring is perpetual. The
GRC Analyst in this phase is responsible for
overseeing the compliance of one or more systems,
coordinating regularly with ISOs and system
administrators, tracking remediation efforts,
supporting ongoing audit activities, and ensuring
that leadership always has an accurate picture
of the system's current risk posture.

This is also where the communication skills of
a GRC Analyst matter most. Translating ongoing
security status into clear, actionable information
for both technical teams and non-technical
leadership is a daily requirement in Step 7.

---

## Proactive vs. Reactive Monitoring

The shift from reactive to proactive security is
one of the most important trends in the GRC field
right now. Reactive monitoring waits for something
to go wrong and then responds. Proactive monitoring
uses continuous data, threat intelligence, and
regular control reviews to identify and address
risks before they materialize into incidents.
Organizations that invest in proactive monitoring
programs are significantly better positioned to
maintain their ATO and protect their systems
over the long term.

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Continuous Monitoring | An ongoing program of security activities designed to maintain awareness of a system's security posture in real time |
| POAM | Plan of Action and Milestones — tracks known weaknesses and the steps being taken to remediate them |
| Vulnerability Management | The process of identifying, classifying, prioritizing, and remediating security vulnerabilities |
| Patch Management | The process of keeping systems current with security updates and patches |
| ATO Revocation | The withdrawal of a system's authorization to operate due to unacceptable risk |
| Continuous Monitoring Plan | A document that defines how and how often security controls will be monitored for a specific system |

---

## The RMF Is a Cycle Not a Checklist

Step 7 does not end. It feeds back into the
beginning. Changes to the system, new threats,
and evolving organizational requirements may
trigger a return to earlier steps. A significant
system change may require recategorization. A
new regulatory requirement may require additional
control selection. The RMF is designed to be
iterative and adaptive because the threat
landscape that organizations operate in is
never static.
