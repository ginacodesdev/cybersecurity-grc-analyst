## Overview

The system has been defined, categorized, controlled,
implemented, and assessed. Everything now comes to
a single decision point. Step 6 of the RMF is
Authorize and it is where the Authorizing Official
(AO) reviews everything that has been produced and
determines whether the system is approved to operate.
No system can officially go live without this
authorization. This is the boss fight of the RMF.

---

## Who Is the Authorizing Official

The Authorizing Official is a senior organizational
leader (typically an executive or senior manager)
who has the authority and accountability to formally
accept the risks associated with operating the system.
The AO is not a technical role. Their job is to
weigh the mission value of the system against the
residual risks that remain after all controls have
been implemented and assessed, and to make a
business decision about whether to proceed.

---

## What the AO Reviews

The AO receives a complete authorization package
that includes everything produced throughout the
RMF process. The key components of that package
are as follows:

| Document | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| System Security and Privacy Plan (SSPP) | The comprehensive record of the system's security posture |
| Security Assessment Report (SAR) | The formal findings from Step 5 |
| Plan of Action and Milestones (POAM) | Outstanding weaknesses and remediation timelines |
| Risk Assessment | The overall risk profile of the system |

The AO reviews this package and makes a determination
about whether the benefits of operating the system
outweigh the risks that remain.

---

## The Three Authorization Decisions

The AO has three options when reviewing the
authorization package:

| Decision | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| Authorization to Operate (ATO) | The system is approved to operate. All significant risks have been addressed or accepted. |
| Conditional ATO | The system may operate under specific conditions. Outstanding issues must be resolved within a defined timeframe, typically six months to one year. |
| Denial of Authorization | The system is not approved to operate. Risks are too significant or the authorization package is too incomplete to support a favorable decision. |

A conditional ATO is not a failure but it is not
a free pass either. It comes with specific
requirements and a timeline. If those requirements
are not met the conditional ATO can be revoked
and the system may be required to shut down.

---

## What Happens After Authorization Is Denied

A denial is not necessarily permanent but it does
mean that the system cannot operate until the
issues driving the denial are resolved. The
organization must address the concerns raised
by the AO, update the relevant documentation,
and resubmit for authorization. This is a costly
and time-consuming outcome which is why the
quality of work in Steps 1 through 5 matters
so much.

---

## The Role of the GRC Analyst in Step 6

The GRC Analyst's primary responsibility in Step 6
is to ensure that the authorization package is
complete, accurate, and clearly organized before
it reaches the AO. This means verifying that all
required documents are present and current,
that POAMs are well-documented with realistic
milestones, and that the overall risk picture
is communicated in terms that a non-technical
executive can understand and act on.

The GRC Analyst may also be asked to brief the
AO directly or to respond to questions about
the system's risk posture. Being able to explain
technical risk in plain business language is
essential at this stage. An AO who cannot
understand what they are being asked to accept
is unlikely to sign off with confidence.

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Authorizing Official (AO) | The senior leader responsible for making the authorization decision |
| Authorization to Operate (ATO) | Formal approval for a system to operate within an accepted level of risk |
| Conditional ATO | Authorization granted with specific conditions that must be met within a defined timeframe |
| Authorization Package | The complete set of documents reviewed by the AO to support the authorization decision |
| Residual Risk | The risk that remains after all security controls have been implemented |

---

## Connection to the Next Step

An ATO is not the finish line, it is the starting
line for Step 7. Once a system is authorized to
operate it enters continuous monitoring and the
work of maintaining its security posture begins
in earnest. The ATO can be revoked at any time
if the system's risk posture deteriorates beyond
acceptable levels. Adversaries do not care when
the ATO was signed. They care whether the guard
is down right now.
