## What Is the RMF

The Risk Management Framework is a structured process
developed by the National Institute of Standards and
Technology (NIST) that helps organizations manage
cybersecurity risk in a consistent, repeatable, and
documentable way. It is widely used across federal
agencies and is increasingly adopted in the private
sector as a gold standard for security program management.

For a GRC Analyst, the RMF is not just a framework to
study, it is the operational backbone of the work.
Understanding each step, what it produces, and how it
connects to the next is essential to performing the role
effectively.

---

## The Seven Steps

| Step | Name | Purpose |
|------|------|---------|
| 1 | Prepare | Define the system, stakeholders, and risk context |
| 2 | Categorize | Assess the system's impact level using the CIA Triad |
| 3 | Select | Choose the appropriate security controls |
| 4 | Implement | Put the selected controls into place |
| 5 | Assess | Verify that controls are working as intended |
| 6 | Authorize | Obtain official approval to operate the system |
| 7 | Monitor | Continuously track and maintain security posture |

---

## Why This Matters in Practice

The RMF is not a one-time checklist. It is a continuous
cycle. A system that receives an Authorization to Operate
(ATO) in step six does not stop being subject to the
framework; it enters step seven and stays there
indefinitely. Threats evolve, systems change, and
controls that worked last year may not be sufficient
today. A GRC Analyst's job is to keep that cycle running
smoothly across every system they are responsible for.
