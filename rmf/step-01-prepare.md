## Overview

Before a single control is selected or a single document
is written, an organization must lay the groundwork for
everything that follows. Step 1 of the RMF is Prepare
and it exists to answer the foundational questions that
every information system requires before it can be
properly secured and managed.

---

## Key Questions to Answer in Step 1

Before standing up an information system, the following
questions must be addressed:

| Question | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| What is the information system? | Defines the scope and boundaries of what is being secured |
| Who are the stakeholders? | Identifies system owners, administrators, and those affected by the system |
| What risks may the organization encounter? | Establishes the risk context before controls are selected |
| What resources will the system need? | Determines infrastructure, personnel, and budget requirements |
| What is the system's purpose in relation to organizational goals? | Ensures the system supports the mission and justifies its existence |

---

## What Gets Produced in Step 1

The primary output of Step 1 is the beginning of the
System Security and Privacy Plan (SSPP). This document
will be referenced and updated throughout the entire
system development life cycle. It is not a one-time
deliverable. It is a living document that follows the
system from creation through decommission.

Additional outputs include an initial risk assessment,
identification of key roles and responsibilities, and
a preliminary understanding of the data types the
system will process, store, or transmit.

---

## The Role of the GRC Analyst in Step 1

A GRC Analyst plays a critical role in the preparation
phase. While system owners and technical teams define
what the system does, the GRC Analyst ensures that the
risk and compliance context is established from the
very beginning. This includes identifying applicable
regulatory requirements, determining which frameworks
govern the system, and making sure that security and
privacy considerations are built into the system
before development begins rather than bolted on
afterward.

This proactive approach is the difference between
security by design and security by reaction. Getting
Step 1 right makes every subsequent step more
efficient, more accurate, and significantly less
painful.

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| Information System | Any combination of hardware, software, and data that processes, stores, or transmits information |
| System Owner | The individual responsible for the overall operation and security of the system |
| System Security and Privacy Plan (SSPP) | The master document that defines how a system is secured and how privacy requirements are met |
| Stakeholder | Any individual or group with an interest in the system's operation or security |
| Risk Context | The environment of threats, vulnerabilities, and organizational factors that shape security decisions |

---

## Connection to the Next Step

Everything established in Step 1 feeds directly into
Step 2 — Categorize. The data types identified, the
stakeholders defined, and the risk context established
here will all be used to determine the system's impact
level and drive control selection in the steps that
follow.
